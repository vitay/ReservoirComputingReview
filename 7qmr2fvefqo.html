\cite{Zheng_2014}:  Synfire chains

        - investigation of synfire chain formation via plastic reservoir
        - Model properties: SORN, see \cite{Lazar_2009}
        - Self-organizing properties of reservoir lead to the development of feed-forward motifs
        - These feed-forward motifs enable the emergence of synfire chains or synfire rings (if circular)
        - Conclusions:suggests
                a) feed-forward motifs as developed by the reservoir supports synfire chain generation as observed in the brain
                b) such feed-forward motifs can be learned via basic (unsupervised) self-organizing mechanisms (i.e. the 3 plasticity mechanisms from an initially unstructured network

\cite{Kim_2018}: Recurrent spiking dynamics

         - Model:
                 * N randomly, sparsely connected quadratic IF / theta neurons
                 * Synapses are modeled as leaky integrators
                 * Connections are trained via recursive least-squares (update every delta_t ms; minmization of MSE + L2 norm of weights)
         - Trained network to reproduce target trajectories of post-synaptic drives / pre-synaptic spiking activity at each network unit
         - Targets were various functions of oscillatory (sinusiodal or non-sinusiodal) or chaotic responses to a short extrinsic input stimulus
        - Model was able to learn multiple input-output relationships regarding its recurrent spiking activity
        - results were also obtained with LIF/Izhikevich neurons
        - Spike rate recordings could be fit, if model size was at least 3 times larger than the number of neurons recorded from (~200)
        - A random network with balanced excitation and inhibition is a canonical model for a cortical circuit that produces asynchronous single unit activity (Sompolinsky et al., 1988; van Vreeswijk et al.,              1996; Renart et al., 2010; Ostojic, 2014; Rosenbaum et al., 2017)
        - "Most previous research on learning in recurrent neural networks focused on training the network outputs to perform useful computations and subsequently analyzed the recurrent activity in
             comparison with measured neuron activity (Sussillo and Abbott, 2009; Sussillo et al., 2015; Sussillo and Barak, 2013; Wang et al., 2018; Chaisangmongkon et al., 2017; Remington et al., 2018)"
        - "It would be of interest to find out whether more biologically plausible learning schemes, such as reward-based learning (Fiete and Seung, 2006; Hoerzer et al., 2014; Miconi, 2017) can lead to                         similar performance"
        - Conclusions:
                  a) If the input is quasi-static (must be slow enough compared to the synaptic time scale) and heterogeneous enough, the reservoir can learn
                       any input-output relationship on its spiking dynamics
                  b) Single neurons in such reservoir structures can then learn to produce highly complex activity patterns by adjusting their incoming connections
                  b) Spiking dynamics in cortical patches may be generated by recurrently connected E-I networks with additional recurrencies to other neural circuits
                  c) Recursive least-squares optimization of the connectivity may conceptually explain which target functions are optimized by structural changes in such networks
